# The Weekly Phalanx: Critical Infrastructure Under Siege and the "Snow" Malware Surge

### TL;DR
*   **Critical Vulnerability:** A 10.0 CVSS critical flaw in Notepad++ has been disclosed, posing a significant risk to local system integrity.
*   **Malware Alert:** A new, sophisticated malware suite dubbed "Snow" is actively targeting organizations via Microsoft Teams social engineering.
*   **Data Breach Wave:** Major players ADT and Itron have confirmed significant data breaches, highlighting ongoing risks to utility and home security sectors.
*   **IoT/OT Fragility:** A deluge of buffer overflow and command injection vulnerabilities in Tenda and Moxa hardware underscores the persistent insecurity of edge and industrial devices.

---

## The Threat Landscape: Malware & Breaches

### Microsoft Teams "Snow" Malware [BleepingComputer]
Threat actor UNC6692 is leveraging Microsoft Teams to deploy a custom, multi-stage malware suite named "Snow." The toolkit includes a browser extension, a tunneler, and a backdoor, indicating a sophisticated approach to persistent access.

**Phalanx thoughts:** The use of collaboration platforms as an initial access vector remains a high-ROI tactic for attackers. Defenders must move beyond traditional email filtering and implement strict governance over external communication and application integration within Teams.*

### Data Breaches: ADT and Itron [BleepingComputer]
Both ADT and utility firm Itron have disclosed unauthorized access to their internal systems. The ADT breach, linked to extortion group ShinyHunters, serves as a stark reminder that even "secure" service providers are prime targets for data exfiltration.

## Vulnerability Spotlight: The "Tenda" Tsunami and Beyond

### Hardware & IoT Vulnerabilities
This week saw a massive influx of vulnerabilities affecting Tenda F456 and HG3 devices, primarily centered around buffer overflows and OS command injection (e.g., CVE-2026-7097, CVE-2026-7096, CVE-2026-7082, CVE-2026-7081, CVE-2026-7080, CVE-2026-7079). Additionally, Moxa Secure Routers are facing critical DoS and credential disclosure risks (CVE-2026-3868, CVE-2026-3867).

**Phalanx thoughts:** The sheer volume of vulnerabilities hitting Tenda hardware suggests a fundamental failure in their secure development lifecycle (SDL). If these devices are present in your environment, they should be treated as untrusted and isolated from critical network segments immediately.*

### Critical Software Flaws
*   **Notepad++ (CVE-2026-3008):** A 10.0 CVSS critical vulnerability allows for memory address leakage or application crashes.
*   **SourceCodester Pharmacy System (CVE-2026-7088, CVE-2026-7087):** Critical SQL injection flaws in the pharmacy sales and inventory system.
*   **Toonflow-app (CVE-2026-7085):** A critical path traversal vulnerability in the `downloadApp` endpoint.

## CISA KEV Updates [TheHackerNews]
CISA has added four new vulnerabilities to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog, including flaws in SimpleHelp, Samsung MagicINFO 9, and D-Link routers. Federal agencies have until May 2026 to remediate, but for private sector practitioners, these should be patched within the next 48 hours.

## Defender Takeaways

1.  **Prioritize KEV Remediation:** If you are running SimpleHelp, Samsung MagicINFO, or D-Link DIR-823X series routers, these must be your top priority. Active exploitation in the wild means your window for proactive patching has already closed.
2.  **Audit Collaboration Tools:** The "Snow" malware campaign highlights the need for stricter controls on Microsoft Teams. Review your "External Access" and "Guest Access" settings, and consider disabling the ability for users to install unapproved third-party apps or extensions.
3.  **Isolate Legacy/IoT Hardware:** Given the surge in Tenda and Moxa vulnerabilities, audit your network for these devices. If they cannot be patched or replaced, move them to an isolated VLAN with no access to the internet or internal production assets.
4.  **Memory Safety Awareness:** With critical flaws like the Notepad++ memory injection, ensure your endpoint detection and response (EDR) tools are configured to monitor for suspicious process memory access patterns, not just file-based signatures.
5.  **Assume Breach for Service Providers:** The ADT/Itron incidents reinforce that third-party risk is your risk. Review your incident response playbooks to ensure you have a clear communication strategy for when a vendor—not just your own infrastructure—is compromised.
